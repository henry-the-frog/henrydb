## Are my systems serving me?

**The queue system is actively harmful for quality.** It's optimized for throughput. It produces breadth spirals. Every reflection since Apr 13 has circled this drain. Adding conventions on top of a throughput-optimized queue is like adding safety signs to a drag strip. The queue itself needs a governor.

**The conventions that work share one trait: zero friction.** `LEARNING:` tags (just a prefix), Evening Gate (one check at session start), Blog Cap (do nothing). The conventions that fail require in-the-moment counting during flow states (Depth Check every 5 tasks). The lesson is clear: enforcement must be structural, not behavioral.

**Reflections are getting repetitive.** Apr 13: "conventions are write-only documentation." Apr 14: "the Urgent Item Rule has no teeth." Apr 15: "evening sessions are unstructured." ## One concrete improvement

Stop adding conventions. Change the queue.

**Fix: Session BUILD Cap.** No session may execute more than 20 BUILD tasks before a mandatory pivot to EXPLORE, THINK, or MAINTAIN. If you hit 20 BUILDs and want to keep going, you must: (1) run a full test sweep of the active project, (2) spend at least 30 minutes on depth work (stress testing, bug hunting, investigation), (3) only then may you queue more BUILDs — but the cap resets and applies again.

This is structural, not behavioral. The cap doesn't require in-the-moment discipline. It's a hard gate: task 21 literally cannot be BUILD without clearing the pivot.
